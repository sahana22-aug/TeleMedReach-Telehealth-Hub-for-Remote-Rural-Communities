Allergic Reactions

Learn how to identify and manage allergic reactions effectively.

Identifying Allergic Reactions

Allergic reactions occur when the immune system reacts to a foreign substance such as pollen, bee venom, pet dander, or certain foods. Symptoms can range from mild to severe and may include hives, itching, swelling, difficulty breathing, and anaphylaxis.

Managing Allergic Reactions

If you suspect an allergic reaction, it is important to act quickly. For mild reactions, antihistamines may help. For severe reactions, such as anaphylaxis, use an epinephrine auto-injector and seek emergency medical help immediately.

Prevention of Allergic Reactions

To prevent allergic reactions, avoid known allergens, carry emergency medication if prescribed, and wear a medical alert bracelet if you have severe allergies. Regularly consult with a healthcare provider to manage and update your allergy action plan.
